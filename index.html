🍊 Blood Sugar Rises (0-4h)
Your body digests your last meal. Blood sugar and insulin levels rise as nutrients are absorbed. Energy is readily available from glucose.
💧 Blood Sugar Drops (4-8h)
Insulin levels begin to fall. Your body finishes processing the last meal and starts transitioning to using stored energy. You may feel initial hunger signals.
🔥 Fat Burning Begins (8-12h)
With insulin low, your body increasingly turns to fat stores for energy. Glycogen reserves in the liver start depleting. Fat oxidation ramps up significantly.
💜 Autophagy Activates (12-16h)
Cellular cleanup begins! Your cells start recycling damaged components. Human Growth Hormone increases. Ketone production accelerates for brain fuel.
💚 Catabolic Phase (16-20h)
Deep fat burning and cellular repair intensify. Inflammation markers decrease. Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) increases, supporting brain health.
🌟 Deep Renewal (20-24h)
Maximum autophagy and cellular regeneration. Immune system gets a significant boost. Stem cell regeneration may begin. Your body is in deep healing mode.
🍽️ Breaking Your Fast Guide
💧 Start With Liquids (first 15 min)▼
  • Warm water with lemon — gentle on the stomach
  • Bone broth — rich in minerals and collagen
  • Herbal tea — chamomile or ginger to soothe digestion
🥑 Light Foods (15-30 min)▼
  • Cooked vegetables — steamed or sautéed for easy digestion
  • Avocado — healthy fats that are gentle on the gut
  • Small handful of nuts — almonds or walnuts
  • Plain yogurt — probiotics to support gut health
🍗 Full Meal (45-60 min)▼
  • Lean protein — chicken, fish, eggs, or tofu
  • Complex carbs — sweet potato, brown rice, quinoa
  • Healthy fats — olive oil, avocado, nuts
🚫 Foods to Avoid▼
  • Sugary foods — candy, pastries, soda
  • Processed foods — chips, fast food
  • Fried foods — heavy on the digestive system
  • Alcohol — dehydrating and hard on an empty stomach
💡 Pro Tips▼
  • Eat slowly — give your body time to adjust
  • Chew well — aids digestion significantly
  • Stay hydrated — continue drinking water with your meal
  • Listen to your body — stop when you feel satisfied, not full
